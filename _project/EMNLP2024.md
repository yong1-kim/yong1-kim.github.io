**MP2D: An Automated Topic Shift Dialogue Generation Framework Leveraging Knowledge Graphs**

Yerin Hwang, **Yongil Kim**, Yunah Jang, Jeesoo Bang, Hyunkyung Bae, Kyomin Jung

**Abstract:**
Despite advancements in on-topic dialogue systems, effectively managing topic shifts within dialogues remains a persistent challenge, largely attributed to the limited availability of training datasets. To address this issue, we propose Multi-Passage to Dialogue (MP2D), a data generation framework that automatically creates conversational question-answering datasets with natural topic transitions. By leveraging the relationships between entities in a knowledge graph, MP2D maps the flow of topics within a dialogue, effectively mirroring the dynamics of human conversation. Through quantitative and qualitative experiments, we demonstrate MP2D's efficacy in generating dialogue with natural topic shifts. Furthermore, this study introduces a novel benchmark for topic shift dialogues, TS-WikiDialog, and we showcase the performance improvements of models trained on datasets generated by MP2D across diverse topic shift dialogue tasks.
